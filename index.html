Supreme Mathematics

A 5% nation lesson

According to "The Five Percenter "Way of Life" by Felicia Miyakawa, five percenter men and women have committed the numbers (in reference to Supreme Mathematics) and their meanings to memory. The numbers being 0-9, five percenters will use these numbers to "creatively show and prove facts and ideas" (pg 200). These numbers can arise anywhere, but for the purpose of this website, we will display the best usage of each number within the song titles itself (not limited to just 5 percenters). To the right is each number and its respective meaning.



0: Cipher



3005, Childish Gambino

decade: 2010s

"3005" is a captivating track by Childish Gambino, the stage name of multi-talented artist Donald Glover. Released in 2013 as part of his album "Because the Internet," the song blends introspective lyrics with an infectious beat, creating a sonic landscape that resonates with listeners. The title itself, "3005," hints at a distant future, perhaps suggesting a timeless quality to the emotions explored within the song.

In "3005," Gambino delves into themes of loneliness, introspection, and the fleeting nature of relationships. The chorus, with its haunting repetition of "No matter what you say or what you do, when I'm alone I'd rather be with you," encapsulates the paradox of yearning for connection while simultaneously feeling isolated. The track's melancholic undertones are juxtaposed with its upbeat tempo, creating a poignant juxtaposition that reflects the complexities of human emotions. Overall, "3005" stands as a testament to Gambino's prowess as both a lyricist and a producer, leaving a lasting impression on listeners with its raw honesty and infectious rhythm.

2: Wisdom



The Heart Part 2, Kendrick Lamar

decade: 2010s

The Heart series by Kendrick Lamar is a journey through his life and career serving as benchmarks for an emotional ride of each of his music. Specifically part 2 is Kdot rapping about his city of Compton, having moments where he prioritizes genuine emotion over simply stringing impressive lyrical content. He aims to convey raw emotion and connect deeply with his audience through his lyrics. Wisdom is the ability to make sensible decisions and give good advice because of the experience you have.

Because of Kendricks upbringing he is able to display to the listener what he has been through and how it currently influences his decisions, even making a connection with God in the process. “I ain’t perfect / I ain’t seen too many churches or know them testament verses”, Kendrick now defines himself as Christian, and this is early in his career but he acknowledges his imperfections and lack of religious upbringing showing self-awareness and humility. “My uncle doing life inside prison; he wasn’t wrapped too tight / He told me ‘Rap about life, not rap n****s” he provides wisdom, literal advice from his incarcerated uncle recognizing his own importance of rapping about real-life experiences. The heart series allows listeners to better connect with Kdot on an emotional level, “ Im tryna purchase my momma dem’ Benzes / Occupying my time with riches, justifying my time ambitions / Just to coincide, just to go inside, plus we idolize why we living”. Here we see his motivations for success, displaying his material desires while also questioning the deeper meaning and purpose behind them.

Whether boasting about achievements, asserting their prowess, or issuing warnings, each line is delivered with boldness and assertiveness to enphasizes their relentless display of power and dominance. From the laughter signaling their success to the descriptions of weapons and confrontations, each lyric revolves around power.

7: God



Thugz Mansion [7 Remix], 2Pac

decade: 2000s

Overall, The message of Thugz Mansion displays themes of struggle, resilience and the longing for a sense of peace in the face of adversity. The metaphorical “Thug Mansion” is where individuals can refuge from the harsh realities of being African American growing up on the streets. One of the most meaningful songs by 2Pac, talks about how he would rest in peace and eventually find happiness where all troubles from his life come to an end and hoping to find a place of peace after he dies.

However, the 7 remix is a more soulful version of the mix. As this song was released posthumously the number 7 is much more significant. Named 7 remix because it was mixed by a producer “7 Aurelius” has a deeper meaning as he is notably a follower of God. Also known as 7 Channel, it would not be surprising if 7 was actually referred to the Supreme numbers as they specifically chose this number for the posthumous remix. Although his definitely doesn't mention God, these religious themes are very reinforced. It has very strong messages and the remix re-emphasizes this message. Notable lyrics are ”Dear mama, don’t cry, your baby boy’s doin’ good / Tell the homies I’m in Heaven and they ain’t got hoods.”
